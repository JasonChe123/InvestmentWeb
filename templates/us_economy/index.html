10-Year Treasury Constant Minus 2-Year Treasury Constant Maturity (T10Y2Y)
Negative values are considered as a prediction of recession.
Study of Inverted Yield Curve (T10Y2Y) vs S&P500 From 1988 to 2010 (Log Scale)
Theory

The long-term yield is generally higher than the short-term yield. While long-term yield drops below short-term yield, the demand of long-term bonds is increasing and the demand of short-term bonds is decreasing. Investors must have a strong belief in an impending economic downturn, where long-term safety becomes more appealing.

Criticism

The time lag between an inversion and a recession is variable, ranging from a few months to over a year, making it challenging to act on the signal.

Conclusion

The inverted yield curve is a powerful but imperfect tool for forecasting economic trends. While it has historically been a reliable recession predictor, investors should use it alongside other economic indicators, such as unemployment rates, consumer confidence, and corporate earnings, to form a comprehensive market outlook.
